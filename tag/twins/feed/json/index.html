Turns out there is a physical trait of identical twins that isn’t identical. Fingerprints, here is why:

\nBecause fingerprints are influenced by environmental factors during intrauterine life.

Although very similar, the fingerprints of identical twins are nevertheless unique because they are not completely determined by genetics: they are an example of phenotype, which refers to the physical characteristics of an individual that are determined by the interaction of genes and the environment.\n

\n
