Chrome 47 has support for mediaDevices.enumerateDevices():

\n

\nModern browsers make it possible to select input and output devices including cameras, microphones and speakers.

\n

For example:
\n– On a phone, select the front or rear-facing camera.
\n– On a laptop, choose the internal speakers or a speaker connected by Bluetooth.
\n– For a video chat, choose internal or external microphone or camera.\n

\n

This has been available in Firefox since version 39 and is part of the W3C “Media Capture and Streams” draft.

\n

I can see this getting lots of play in the “native vs. web” arguments.
