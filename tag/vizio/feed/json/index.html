Compare http://www.apple.com/privacy/:

\n

\nOur business model is very straightforward: We sell great products. We don\u2019t build a profile based on your email content or web browsing habits to sell to advertisers. We don\u2019t \u201cmonetize\u201d the information you store on your iPhone or in iCloud. And we don\u2019t read your email or your messages to get information to market to you. Our software and services are designed to make our devices better. Plain and\u00a0simple.\n

\n

With http://www.vizio.com/privacy:

\n

\nVIZIO will share Viewing Data, together with the IP address associated with the corresponding VIZIO television, with limited third parties with whom we have specifically partnered. These third parties may combine this information with other information about devices associated with that IP address, in order to customize the advertisements displayed on those other devices.\n

\n

Part of the issue here is expectations of privacy. We’ve had televisions around for decades, during which time tracking hasn’t been an issue, first because of technology then because of laws that were setup consistent with privacy expectations. From ProPublica’s “Own a Vizio Smart TV? It\u2019s Watching You”:

\n

\nCable TV companies and video rental companies are prohibited by law from selling information about the viewing habits of their customers. However, Vizio says that those laws – the Video Privacy Protection Act and cable subscriber protections – don\u2019t apply to its business.\n

\n

Time to decide if we still expect a level of privacy from our “smart” televisions.
