During November the Utah Broadband Project is having a consultant run a mobile broadband driving test:

\n

\nThis November,\u00a0Isotrope LLC, under contract with the Utah Broadband Project, will conduct a mobile drive test on 6,000 miles of roads in Utah. During the drive test, Isotrope will collect real-time mobile broadband wireless coverage and speed information. All mobile wireless broadband data over cellular, PCS, LTE, and IDEN frequencies and protocols will be collected.\n

\n

The drive test map is hard to zoom in on, but it does look like the proposed route comes with in just a block or two of my house. I’m curious to see what the results show in terms of signal strength from the major wireless providers.
