\nThe smallest pipe is a quarter-inch in length. More than 8,000 pipes were added to the Organ between 1911 and 1917, and from 1924 to 1930 an additional 10,000 pipes were installed, bringing the total number of pipes today to 28,500.\n

\nThe pipes are laid out across five floors, with the sections situated as follows:

\n

– 2nd floor south \u2013 Main Pedal 32\u2032, Lower Swell, Great, Percussions
\n – 3rd floor south \u2013 Main Pedal, Chorus, Upper Swell, Choir/Enclosed Great, Solo, Vox Humana Chorus
\n – 4th floor south \u2013 String
\n – 4th floor west \u2013 Orchestral (adjacent to String)
\n – 7th floor south \u2013 Major Chimes, Ethereal, Chinese Gong
\n – 7th floor north \u2013 Echo

\n

The 32\u2032 Wood Open, 32\u2032 Diaphone, and 32\u2032 Metal Diapason pipes run the length of a little more than 2 stories, beginning on the second floor.\n
